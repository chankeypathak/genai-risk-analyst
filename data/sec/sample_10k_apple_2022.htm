Item 1A. Risk Factors

The Company’s operations and financial results are subject to various risks and uncertainties, including those described below. You should carefully consider these risks before making an investment decision regarding the Company’s securities. If any of these risks actually occur, the Company’s business, results of operations, financial condition and stock price could be materially adversely affected.

  • Litigation and Regulatory Risks: The Company is subject to various claims, investigations and legal proceedings, including those relating to intellectual property, antitrust, consumer protection, privacy, and employment matters. Adverse outcomes in these matters could result in significant monetary damages or injunctive relief against the Company, and could adversely affect the Company’s business and reputation.
  • Product Liability: The Company faces risks related to product liability claims, which could result in significant costs and harm to its reputation.
  • International Operations: The Company’s international operations expose it to additional risks, including litigation and regulatory actions in foreign jurisdictions.

For a more complete discussion of these and other risks, see the Company’s filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

Item 3. Legal Proceedings

The Company is involved in various legal proceedings arising in the ordinary course of business. While the Company does not believe that the outcome of these proceedings will have a material adverse effect on its financial condition, the results of litigation are inherently unpredictable.
